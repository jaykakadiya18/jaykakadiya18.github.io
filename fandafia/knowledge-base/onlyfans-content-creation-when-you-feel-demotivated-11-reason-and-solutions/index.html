OnlyFans Content Creation When You Feel Demotivated : 11 Reasons and Solutions

Estimated Reading Time: 3 min 06 sec

Tired, drained, confused, wanting to give up, not feeling like it. Have you gotten to this point? If you have, realize and accept that such symptoms of demotivation are normal. Many other female models share in your current state of demotivation. The solution involves analyzing the cause of demotivation and finding the correct approach to reverse the state allowing you to keep creating content. 

Creating and running a successful OnlyFans business as a part-time or full-time venture requires undivided attention and focus. Despite the requirement, at times the zeal to keep going dies down and you lose interest due to various reasons such as having to work and create OnlyFans content, or your returns not aligning with your expectations. 

If you let demotivation win, you will lose so many subscribers due to inconsistency. Your subscribers get bored having to wait for the next piece. Bear in mind that your subscribers have paid for your exclusive content; therefore, they have the right to complain. Continued complaints due to you keeping them waiting may tarnish your brand because your subscribers feel cheated out of their money. Many of them eventually unsubscribe because they are not receiving the value they used to get. 

Trying to keep creating content even when you feel demotivated may harm the quality of your content. Demotivation puts you in a state of despair and at times confusion; therefore, causing you to produce poor quality content when you force yourself to create content. Being inconsiderate of what is causing your current state of demotivation does not work because your business relies on you following a clearly defined strategy and making well thought out decisions. 

If your urge to produce OnlyFans content has died down, the enthusiasm to serve your subscribers has faded, find solace in this article as it gives you insights into what may be causing your demotivation with clearly elaborated solutions. Avoid losing your subscribers and proclaim your motivated self by going through each outlined reason, spotting and recording your triggers, and gradually working to correct the issue and eventually serve your subscribers with quality and valuable content.

1. You Lack Enough Time to Create Content 

When you are creating content on a part-time basis, you might be overwhelmed by other responsibilities such as work. Having a physically or mentally demanding job alongside your OnlyFans business may blow you off course when the job demands increase leaving you with little time to create content. 

Squeezing in content creation into your busy after-work schedule may be impossible due to mental and physical fatigue. Fatigue demotivates you because you can no longer focus and feel like resting to re-energize rather than creating content. 

If you are a full-time OnlyFans content creator, poor time management might also put you off making you feel like you cannot keep going. You might have a schedule but decide to postpone most of your plans as declared within the schedule.

Continued procrastination leaves you creating content at the last minute, making you feel demotivated especially when things do not work out as you expected and when the deadline catches up with you. Not delivering content to your subscribers based on the schedule you have created demotivates you especially when you think of the repunctuations such as some of the subscribers not purchasing your content because you delivered late. 

If you realize that time management is causing you to lose interest in running your OnlyFans business, do the following:

  • Make a work calendar that includes your rituals and routines 

Despite having an OnlyFans business, there are other general tasks you have to undertake. OnlyFans might be a side hustle to you, helping you generate extra income to sustain your needs. Therefore, to help with your time management problem, you must analyze your daily, weekly, monthly, and yearly OnlyFans tasks, and record them for easier prioritization. 

Monthly and yearly tasks are long-term based while daily and weekly ones are short-term based. Therefore, you are required to first record your daily tasks, analyze them and determine which ones will stretch to the weekly, monthly, or yearly record. This helps you to have a probability-tied list. Ensure the list includes your rituals and routines that you cannot do without. 

Once you have the list. Input every task into a work calendar with precise dates as to when the tasks should be accomplished. You do not have to be so precise with the dates, approximate and record. A work calendar acts as a reference point giving you peace of mind once you start creating varied weekly schedules. 

Content creation is a part of the other tasks that go into running a successful OnlyFans business such as promoting your business. Ensure every task is well designated within a weekly schedule to have an easier time tracking your progress. Also, include your other jobs within the work calendar so that you do not overwhelm yourself. 

  • Make a to-do list 

From your weekly schedule, with reference to the day you are in, come up with a to-do list. A to-do list is daily based and should still have a record of your rituals and routines to reduce the chances of you having to sacrifice your work or OnlyFans business time. Make a to-do list outlining all your day’s activities.

After writing the list, prioritize the tasks to have an easier time accomplishing each task. If OnlyFans content creation falls within the specific day for which you are creating a to-do list and it is a priority, create bulk content. Creating bulk content helps you post consistently without having to sacrifice the time allocated to the other weekly tasks. 

You should also create bulk content for other social media platforms. Also, repurpose the content for other social media platforms when necessary. The aim here is to ensure that the hours set aside for content creation are utilized to the fullest. You are less likely to be demotivated because you know that your content is ready and your OnlyFans business does not have to suffer despite you not having enough time. 

  • Define time limits and diligently work within them 

After prioritizing your tasks, assign each of them a time limit and ensure you work within those limits. Time limits are supposed to keep you alert and focused because your mind knows it has a specific amount of time to accomplish a specific task. Limits discipline your mind, motivating you to produce more content when the time comes. 

  • Incorporate a motivation schedule into your daily to-do lists 

Focusing on content creation without a rewarding system in place leads to boredom. Allocate a reward that you grant yourself once you have completed a specific set of tasks. Rewarding oneself motivates you to keep accomplishing more tasks. The rewards must not be tied to monetary spending, you can decide to relax and watch a movie as a reward. 

2. You Have Not Strategized 

Sometimes, you might assume strategizing because you feel like you are not a professional OnlyFans creator. At first, working without a strategy might work but eventually you will end up confused and demotivated when you reach a dead end and do not know what to do or where you made a mistake. The time you take to solve a problem that arose due to you not strategizing puts you off because it involves rigorous backtracking and recording of your activities. 

Before you start creating any piece of OnlyFans content, you must strategize to have a clearly defined purpose and get your mind organized. Purpose gives you the drive required to accomplish every task that goes into producing quality content while organization makes it easy for you to track and monitor your work to derive what went well and what needs to be changed. 

In case you realize that your demotivation to create content is caused by a lack of strategy, do not keep creating content; rather, take a break. After the break, start analyzing what you have put together while recording each step you went through so that you can have a vivid idea of the objectives and target audience of your content. Once you have every detail put together, organize the data and come up with a strategy so that you can continue creating content. 

3. You are Trying out More than One OnlyFans Content Type 

As an expert or beginner OnlyFans content creator, you might be tempted to try out more than one niche at a go so that you bring in more income or avoid failing. This form of multitasking, despite the reason, has a high chance of causing failure. You might have a working strategy but once everything starts crumpling, you become demotivated because you feel the extra burden of failing twice. 

If creating more than one content type triggers your demotivation due to confusion or feeling like a failure, drop one content category and focus on one. You must train your mind to focus because it might start tempting you to go back to your initial state of creating content for two niches at a go. 

Avoid creating content you are not comfortable with such as NSFW “not safe for work” content because you feel it will be easier to make money. Align your content creation category choice with what you love doing. Doing so motivates you to keep producing content. 

Additionally, clearly define your content creation boundaries to prevent clashes between you and your subscribers. Boundaries help your subscribers tame their emotions and respect you. Lack of boundaries might lead to subscribers requesting for custom content you cannot produce. If you choose to produce content you are not comfortable making to please your audience, you will have a hard time convincing them that you can no longer make such content. 

Continued production of content that does not sit well with your values might discourage you from creating more OnlyFans content, especially when your audience loves the content you despise the most. Therefore, to avoid demotivation due to losing subscribers who felt like they could not get what they wanted, have a clear outline of your content creation boundaries.

4. You Keep Checking Your OnlyFans Statistics Regularly 

Observing your OnlyFans and social media statistics is a crucial part of business management enabling you to devise beneficial strategies. But constantly checking on statistics can demotivate you when your expected results do not show. If you find yourself regularly checking your statistics, get something else to do like creating more content.

Rather than randomly checking your statistics, you can schedule when you will check your statistics. This move helps you calm down and keep creating content without worrying about your subscriber’s response to the content you uploaded to OnlyFans and other social media platforms. 

Another demotivating form of statistics is your notifications. Especially when you are creating content and receive random negative comments as a notification. Once you read through the negative comments, you might feel emotionally hurt and decide to stop creating more content. 

When you receive negative comments and feel demotivated, toggle off your notifications and keep creating content. Keep in mind that negative comments are supposed to help you improve. Therefore, you should toggle off notifications and come back to read them later to learn what your audience thinks suits them better. Doing this trains your brain to receive negative comments in a rather positive manner as opposed to giving up. 

5. You Do Not Take Breaks 

The best way to make money on OnlyFans is to create and post content consistently, respond to your subscriber’s requests in time, and to keep monitoring what is happening by going through analytics. Doing all these continuously without a break might lead to a burnout which might utterly affect your health. 

If you realize that you start feeling generally tired mentally, at times physically, despite wanting to create more content, you are experiencing a burnout. A burnout can take a toll on you especially when you want to go live and your mental state is unstable. Therefore, the only way to deal with demotivation brought about by lack of breaks is by scheduling breaks and having fun once it’s time to take a break. 

There are so many activities you can do during your break but you should not undertake a task that is physically and mentally demanding like weight lifting. Go out and watch a movie, take a walk in the park, meditate or listen to soothing music. Avoid going for a ride as it might put you in danger. 

During your break time, avoid thinking about anything related to your OnlyFans business or work. You can nap and proceed to undertake other activities within your breaktime. The best option is to go out and socialize or call someone. Interacting with other people takes your mind off your OnlyFans responsibilities allowing you to relax. 

After your break time, you will realize that you are re-energized and motivated to create content and accomplish other tasks such as promoting your content on other social media platforms or going live. To power your after-breaktime moment, you can play soothing background music to help you focus, motivating you further.

6. You Are Not Listening to Your Body and Missing Key Mood Changes 

Being strict to oneself helps you accomplish so much in a few hours, days, months, or years. Though, being too strict on yourself might harm you because it blinds you from what is really happening within your body system and how your mood changes. 

You can be happy and suddenly feel an overwhelming wave of sadness. You are not to blame because you are not in control of most of the events that occur in your life, but you are in control of how you respond to your body system and mood changes. If you are in a sad mood or feel sick and still decide to produce content to sustain your OnlyFans earnings, you are likely to become demotivated because you are pushing yourself too much and receiving poor results. 

If your mood changes and you feel like you have lost interest in creating content and would love to do something else, kindly do what you feel like doing and create content later. Following your mood changes helps you stabilize quickly rather than fighting them. It is like a wave and if you calmly align yourself to the wave, you will sail smoothly. 

If your demotivation is being caused by you pushing yourself to create more content despite being sick, take time to go and see a doctor. To avoid losing your subscribers, inform them that you are not feeling well and give them an incentive to stay back. For example, you can decide to give all your subscribers a monthly subscription cut while you recover. If possible, you can request for help from your subscribers. The genuine ones will help because they want to see you back on your feet creating content for them. 

Keep in mind that you can easily alter your work calendar, schedules, and other plans, but you cannot ignore your health and mood changes because the situation might worsen leading to the crash of your OnlyFans business. Therefore, love yourself to reduce the chances of demotivation. 

7. You Are Comparing Yourself to Other OnlyFans Creators

OnlyFans has more than 1.5 million creators all striving to clinch the attention of more than 170 million users. Inevitably, there are creators earning more than you and there are some underneath your rank. It is normal to be disadvantaged at some point, but if you let it get to you, then demotivation will kick in. 

If you are watching the results of other creators and feeling demotivated because they are doing better than you, remember why you started creating OnlyFans content and your mission. Your mission and aim usually differ from what other creators want and you cannot judge your success based on their results. That is why you should never compare yourself to others. 

Rather than comparing yourself to other creators, network with them and learn from their mistakes. Teamwork and learning from each other will benefit and help you grow quicker. Reach out to creators who are raking in more income than you and propose collaborations. In case you are rejected, do not be discouraged; rather, take time to re-evaluate why the other creators rejected you and how you can send worthwhile pitches to boost the chances of established creators accepting your collaboration proposals. 

Moreover, you can learn from the other creators. Take time to analyze how their posts are presented and the story their feed tales. Established creators have more experience and know what works. Therefore, when you have the chance to interact with their posts, do so and gain useful insights you can use to enhance your content creation strategy. 

8. You Have Set Unrealistic Goals 

Goals help you track your progress and know which moves to implement next. Coming up with vague unattainable goals will discourage you because you cannot determine whether you have attained your goals or not. If you do not define your short-term and long-term goals, you will end up demotivated because you feel lost. You should come up with SMART goals to deal with this issue. SMART goals are specific, measurable, attainable, relevant, and time based. 

You should know the exact objective you want to achieve out of every piece of content you create. Define who the content is for and the objectives. Having a specific subscriber target leads to the creation of a stronger relationship between you and your subscribers because the content you create is valuable to them and serves them better. 

Assign key performance indicators to every goal you create so that you can easily measure the outcome of each. Break down long-term goals into smaller chunks creating short-term goals with specific milestones. Once a milestone is reached, you can use the key performance indicators to measure performance. 

Your goals should be attainable in accordance with the available resources. It is a waste of time defining a goal that stretches your financial budget and yet you had a specific plan that fits the budget. Therefore, when defining goals, consider the available resources. 

Ensure your goals are closely connected to your selected niche and consider your other life responsibilities. They should match your professional and personal values to motivate you further. 

Every goal you create should fit into your schedule and be attainable within a specific time-limit. Break down your goals into smaller tasks that you can assign time to allowing you to accomplish them easily. 

9. You Doubt the Quality of Your Content 

Creating OnlyFans content is both rewarding and unrewarding. When it is unrewarding, you might feel like your content creation technique is failing you and start doubting yourself. You can know that you are doubtful once you start having the fear to post your content or edit your content severally and fail to post. If you keep doing this, you will eventually become demotivated because you look at all your unposted work and feel like a loser. 

To deal with self-doubt, change what you are telling your mind. If you whisper negative thoughts to your mind, it will reciprocate negativity and vice versa. Start by indicating to your mind that it is okay to make mistakes and you can always learn from your mistakes. You can repeatedly do this when you are about to post content on OnlyFans. 

Your subscribers know what they need from you and to improve, you must post and let them show you the way. You should create quality and valuable content, take it through a quality and value checklist to affirm that you are good to go and post. Do not start overthinking about what you can change. 

10. Your Surrounding Environment is Toxic for Content Creation 

The environment you are creating content in can also demotivate you based on your personal view. Some symptoms of a toxic environment that might cause you to feel demotivated include creating content in a poorly lit room, cluttered room, or one with poorly arranged furniture. Additionally, the people surrounding you might be oozing negative energy. 

To change your toxic environment to a more conducive and motivating one, you have to deal with the individual cause of your demotivation. If the room is poorly lit, acquire artificial lights or let in more natural light. Natural light boosts your motivation and mood, helping you keep creating content. 

You can also rearrange your furniture to create an environment you feel more motivated to create content within. If the people in the room make you feel sick, you can ask them to change or get a positivity-oriented team or friends to help you create content

11. At Times, You Cannot Determine What is Demotivating You

Most of the time, there is a reason as to why you are demotivated. But at times you cannot plainly state what is going on. The feeling of not wanting to create content comes out of nowhere and you end up demotivated for no good reason. Sometimes, you might be overwhelmed by more than one of the reasons mentioned in this article making it hard for you to distinguish what solution to use. 

When you feel demotivated and do not know the reason, just get up and do something. With time, you might start spotting the reason causing your demotivation. After all, if you realize it or not, you would have created content and your demotivated self would have changed to one ready to push through with the upcoming projects.
